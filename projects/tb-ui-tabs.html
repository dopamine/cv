Developing new UI elements for Tesco Bank

Problem

While designing brochureware pages for a new high profile product launch, I found that I needed a new UI component.

Something that would draw customers in and encourage them to explore the Clubcard benefits offered by Tesco, in order to put the value of the new product into context.

I produced detailed visuals and interaction specifications to describe the new UI pattern and get it ready for development.

My Process

I quickly settled on horizontal tabs as the best UI pattern to present this complex information in a rich and compelling way, but no similar patterns were available in Tesco Bank’s style guide and repertoire.

I always start by assessing similar patterns already available within our existing style guides or on the live site itself.

If none of them are quite the perfect fit, then I begin creating something that borrows from the existing visual language, to maintain coherence.

For this particular task I also created two new icons following brand icon style guides.

The work had to go through Design Crit (which was standard process at Tesco) &emdash; my preferred option (on the right) was also favoured by the rest of the team, so the decision was made to continue with that one.

At this point (or in fact even before the crit took place) I also validated my work with a Developer, to make sure that it fit in with our current development standards and strategy.

Once I was sure that the wider community is happy that the new UI pattern is suitable, reusable and on-brand, I could proceed to finessing the final design and preparing detailed interaction specs (in code) for the Developers to work from.

See the Pen Horizontal tabs by Zita Szedercsíra (@dopamine) on CodePen.

After handing over these detailed assets, I also spent quite a bit of time sitting with the Developer during the implementation phase, to answer questions, help resolve styling conflicts and integrate the new UI widget with the rest of the site.

The final piece

The new component's primary role was communicating the key benefits of collecting Clubcard points. It was featured prominently during the launch of a new credit product in 2016, and continues to be used to this day on the product page.
