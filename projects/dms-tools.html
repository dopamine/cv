Zita Orbán | Portfolio case study

Automated correspondence analysis tool for Sky

Problem

Between 2011-2014 I managed a team of XSLT developers and looked after a high-availability component (DMS) that batch generated Sky’s automated correspondence.

It was an obscure area nobody outside our team knew, and my workload was hampered by business & technology queries from across the organisation.

Solution

I designed and implemented a reporting framework that initially presents the user with a simple interface serving the most common tasks, but opens up into more complex reports for advanced users.

Along with the tool, I created a presentation aimed at senior stakeholders within Sky, one that I could quickly pull out and present on a tablet in meetings to raise DMSTools’s profile. It summarises the need and the feature set, but I also go into more detail on my process of designing and developing it, and how I iterated the design.

Results

DMSTools quickly became the go-to toolkit for Test Analysts and Business Analysts across the business. My team’s productivity increased significantly as we no longer had to deal with frequent queries from across the business.
